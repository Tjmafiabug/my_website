The Origin Story

Building tools for the next billion creators.

I started coding at 12 because I wanted to change the rules of the games I played. Now, I'm building platforms that change the rules of how we work.

01. Roots

Born in the terminal.

Growing up in the early 2010s, the web wasn't just a utility—it was my playground. While peers were playing sports, I was dissecting open-source repositories and building IRC bots. This wasn't just hobbyist curiosity; it was an obsession with systems.

"Simplicity is the ultimate sophistication, but performance is the ultimate truth."

02. The First Leap

The $1M Mistake.

My first startup failed spectacularly. We had the tech, but no market empathy. It taught me more about building than any success ever could. Credibility isn't built on wins alone, but on the capacity to pivot and persist. That failure became the foundation for everything I've built since.

03. The Mission

Democratizing infrastructure.

We're at a point where the barrier to entry for great ideas shouldn't be technical complexity. My mission is to build the digital landscape that feels human-centric once again, providing the tools for the agile innovator to scale without friction.

"Innovation isn't just about the new; it's about the necessary."

Today, at 24, I sit at the intersection of capital and code. I've spent the last three years advising early-stage founders while scaling my own venture, Founder™. Our focus is singular: reducing the latency between a "what if" and a "live."

The startup ecosystem is often criticized for its noise. My goal is to build the signal. By leveraging Kinetic Minimalist principles in our UI and highly performant architectures in our backends, we're creating a digital landscape that feels human-centric once again.

When I'm not in the terminal, you'll find me exploring the intersection of generative art and algorithmic music, or mentoring the next wave of builders who, like me, started with nothing but a cheap laptop and a curious mind.
